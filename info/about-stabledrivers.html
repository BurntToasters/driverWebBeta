About driver grading and what "Considered a stable driver" means?

Driver ranking:

Driver rankings are based off of my personal experience with using the "Considered a stable driver" Nvidia drivers.

Example:

  • A- - A+ | Best overall experience with drivers

  • B - B+ | Very good overall experience. Some bugs here and there but solid choice.

  • C+ - B- | Ok experience. Too many stutters and bugs to rate higher. Would reccomend a higher rated driver, unless this specific driver is needed for game/software compatibility.

  • C and below | Not good experience. High GPU latency, high amount of stutters and bugs. Personally do not reccomend installing.

  • / Means the driver is pending a grade. Check back later!

    • ⓘ For reference, my current setup is: Ryzen 9 7900X, RTX 4080 SUPER, 64GB DDR5 6000MHz, Win 11 23H2

    What does "Considered a stable driver" mean?

    The stable driver tag I have on some of the driver download pages refers to community feedback and response to newley release drivers. I usually look at reddit threads regarding a new driver release, and if the general outlook is that the driver seems to be working well and is worth an update, I put that specific text next to the driver version with a link to the thread I used to determine adding that tag. Additionally I also take into consideration my personal experience with using said drivers.
